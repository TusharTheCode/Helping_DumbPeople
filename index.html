E-Banking

E-Banking is the use of electronic devices and the internet to access banking services.

Types of E-Banking

1. Internet Banking
Banking services provided through a bank's website.
Services: Fund transfer, bill payment, account balance inquiry.

2. Mobile Banking
Banking services provided through mobile apps.
Services: Money transfer, recharge, bill payment, account management.

3. Phone Banking
Banking services provided through phone calls.
Services: Balance inquiry, account information, customer support.

4. ATM Banking
Banking services provided through Automated Teller Machines using a 4 digit pin (ATMs).
Services: Cash withdrawal, balance inquiry, mini statement, cash deposit.

Advantages of E-Banking
  • Available 24/7
  • Saves Time
  • Easy and Convenient
  • Quick Transactions
  • Reduced Paperwork
Disadvantages of E-Banking
  • Backdoor Attacks
  • Denial of Service (DoS) Attacks
  • Direct Access Attacks

Advantages of E-Shopping

  • Easier to purchase goods from the comfort of your house.
  • 24/7 accessibility
  • Wide variety of products

Disadvantages of E-Shopping

  • Lack of personal touch, E-Shopping lacks real touch and the ability to try the products.
  • Lack of cyber security, some sites are not secure or do not encrypt the credit card data.
  • Transportation cost, some sites charge extra fees for delivering the products.
  • Online fraud, some sites sell items for cheap but customers often recieve low quality products
